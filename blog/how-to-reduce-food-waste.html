5 Practical Ways to Reduce Food Waste in Your Kitchen

Published on March 18, 2026 by Zucchini Team

Food waste is a significant global issue, with millions of tons of perfectly edible food ending up in landfills every year. Not only is this an environmental concern, but it's also a drain on your household budget. At Zucchini, we're dedicated to helping you make the most of what's in your pantry. Here are five practical tips to help you reduce food waste today.

1. Master the Art of Ingredient Matching

One of the biggest reasons food goes to waste is that we buy a specific ingredient for one recipe and then don't know what to do with the leftovers. This is exactly where tools like Zucchini come in. Before you head to the grocery store, enter the ingredients you already have into our recommender to see if you can create a delicious meal without buying anything new.

2. Understand "Use By" vs. "Best Before"

Many people confuse these two labels. "Use By" is about safety—you shouldn't eat food past this date. "Best Before" is about quality—the food is still safe to eat, but it might not be at its peak flavor or texture. Trust your senses: if it looks, smells, and tastes fine, it's likely still good to use.

3. Proper Storage is Key

Storing your food correctly can significantly extend its shelf life. For example, keep your potatoes and onions separate, as onions release gases that can make potatoes sprout faster. Wrap leafy greens in a damp paper towel to keep them crisp, and store herbs like a bouquet of flowers in a small jar of water.

4. Embrace the Freezer

Your freezer is your best friend when it comes to waste reduction. Almost anything can be frozen: bread, milk, herbs (in oil), and even leftover sauces. If you notice a vegetable is starting to look a bit sad, chop it up and freeze it for use in a future soup or stew.

5. Plan Your Meals (Flexibly)

While strict meal planning works for some, it can be hard to stick to. Instead, try "flexible meal planning." Choose 3-4 recipes for the week but leave a couple of nights open for "kitchen sink" meals—dishes like stir-fries, omelets, or pasta bakes that can accommodate whatever random ingredients are left in your fridge.

By making these small changes, you'll be surprised at how much food (and money) you can save. Happy sustainable cooking!
